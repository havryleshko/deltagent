## Comparison

Round 3 improves the measured average from `11.6/15` to `12.6/15`, which lands near the upper half of the planned `12.2–12.8/15` range for this round.

What clearly improved versus round 2:

- Source rendering is now stable. The malformed-source failures from Cases 03, 06, and 07 are fixed, and the Subscription placeholder-source issue in Case 02 no longer contaminates the output format.
- The eval runner completed cleanly with no repeated `Event loop is closed` teardown warning.
- Case 06 now uses the narrower `Vantec` / residual-exposure evidence that was previously left on the table.
- Case 07 keeps the COR and Professional Services linkage while presenting it in a cleaner final structure.

What still holds the score below `13/15` average:

- Some commentary still overstates confidence on future period timing or recovery, especially in Cases 06 and 07.
- Case 02 still misses the insurance-claim detail even though it exists in the fixture; that is now mostly a retrieval/use issue, not a rendering issue.
- Cases 01 and 04 are substantively good, but the remaining gap is mainly richer evidence synthesis and tighter executive register.

## Top 2–3 Recurring Failures

1. Confidence control is still imperfect: some lines still sound more certain about forward timing or recovery than the evidence fully supports.
2. A few cases still leave meaningful fixture detail unused even when source rendering is now correct.
3. Tone is improved but not yet consistently as tight and board-pack-like as the gold commentary.
