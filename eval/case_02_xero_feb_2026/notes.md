# Case 02 — Xero Demo, February 2026

**Purpose:** Tests the agent on two distinct cost overruns with different characters — a deliberate timing shift (campaign moved forward) and an unplanned emergency (HVAC repair). Revenue is slightly above budget but below significance threshold.

**What makes this case interesting:**
- Advertising & Marketing (+67%): Planned campaign moved from March to February. The full Q1 budget was pulled forward, so March will be correspondingly under. The agent should distinguish a deliberate reallocation from an uncontrolled overspend.
- Repairs & Maintenance (+786%): Single emergency callout, unplanned but approved. Classic one-off event — the agent should label it clearly and note the insurance claim.
- Subscriptions are significantly under (-£1,200, -100%) — worth noting but below the abs=500 threshold on the net basis. Watch for whether the agent mentions this.
- Revenue just below significance threshold (+8.4%, +£506) — the agent should NOT treat this as significant.

**Thresholds:** pct=10%, abs=£500

**What a good output looks like:**
- Advertising commentary explains the campaign pull-forward and mentions the net March offset
- Repairs commentary identifies it as a one-off emergency, names the event, notes the insurance claim
- Revenue noted as slightly above plan but not commentary-worthy
- No fabricated reasons for other lines
