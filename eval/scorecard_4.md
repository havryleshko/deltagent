## Comparison

Round 4 improves the measured average from `12.6/15` to `13.4/15`, which is in line with the intended goal for this corrective pass.

What clearly improved versus Round 3:

- Case 02 is the main success: the agent now runs the narrow Gmail follow-up on Repairs and surfaces the insurance-claim detail in both commentary and sources.
- Case 03 improves factual reconciliation by tying the January actual to both the Q4 carry-overs and the two additional inbound deals.
- Case 07 is materially better structured: the output now separates recoverable slippage from permanent loss instead of bundling the full miss into one recovery narrative.
- Case 06 is directionally better on confidence control: it is less forceful than Round 3, even though it still carries some advisory phrasing around forward provision.

What still holds the score below the mid-14 range:

- Tone is still the biggest cross-case limiter. Cases 01, 04, 05, 06, and 07 remain more operational and recommendation-heavy than the gold board-pack style.
- Confidence control is improved but not fully solved. Cases 05, 06, and 07 still contain some forward-looking language that is acceptable but not yet as tight as the gold.
- Evidence richness still limits some lines, especially Case 04 salary explanations and Case 07 downstream COR sourcing.

## Top 2–3 Remaining Failures

1. Tone and compression are still inconsistent versus the gold board-pack register.
2. Forward-looking language is improved but not yet fully disciplined on forecast-sensitive lines.
3. A few cases still need richer corroboration on downstream or secondary lines, especially when only one source type is returned.
