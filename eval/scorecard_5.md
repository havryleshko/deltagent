## Comparison

Round 5 produces a modest but real gain from `13.4/15` to `13.7/15`. The improvements are narrower than Round 4, which is consistent with the remaining issues now being mostly style/control problems rather than major factual misses.

What improved versus Round 4:

- Case 01 is cleaner and now earns stronger source quality credit because both the revenue and Purchases lines stay well-supported while reading tighter overall.
- Case 06 improves materially on confidence control: the forward-budget advice is largely removed and the remaining exposure is framed more cautiously.
- Case 04 and Case 07 now surface thin corroboration honestly instead of overstating certainty, which is directionally correct even when it does not yet lift the score.

What still blocks the move into the mid-14 range:

- Tone compression is still the biggest cross-case limiter. Cases 01, 03, 05, and 07 remain longer than the gold board-pack style, especially on lead lines.
- Single-source downstream commentary still caps quality where corroboration cannot be surfaced, most obviously Case 04 salary lines and Case 07 Cost of Revenue.
- A few post-processing choices are now slightly awkward in their own way, such as duplicated no-context wording in Case 02 and the appended USPTO sentence in Case 06.

## Top Remaining Failures

1. The final style gap is now mostly compression: lead lines still over-explain compared with the gold register.
2. Downstream and headcount lines still lack stronger corroboration in the cases where the fixture genuinely only returns one source family.
3. A small number of deterministic cleanup rules still need refinement so they do not introduce awkward phrasing while tightening tone.
